1.1 Recruitment Procedure

Recruitment is a process of attracting a pool of potential candidates from the labour market to apply for the job, whereas selection is an elimination process to choose the best fit for the job. Recruitment is the process of searching for and attracting qualified candidates to apply for the positions that are available.

1.1.2 The Applicant Databank is a Central Repository of Applicant related Information

The Recruitment Procedure has different options – Direct and Through Requisitions:

  • Direct: The Candidate required for a vacant position in the organization is searched from the Applicant Data Bank and given an offer.
  • Through: The requisition process comprises of manpower requisitions from the organization.

After approval of the requisitions of applicants, candidates are shortlisted from the Applicant Data Bank based on position, experience, skill set, and qualifications as required from the requisition. The shortlisted applicants are then called for an evaluation process based on predefined steps for the respective positions. After clearing the evaluation process, an offer letter is given to the selected applicants.

If the applicant is rejected or put on hold during the evaluation process, the applicant goes back to the Applicant Data Bank with the appropriate status.
